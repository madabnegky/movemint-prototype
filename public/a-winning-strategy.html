The Strategy

Two ways to move
the same number.

Movemint drives ROI for financial institution clients on both sides of the ledger simultaneously. We reduce the cost of running prescreen campaigns, and we increase the revenue those campaigns generate. Neither lever alone is the strategy — the strategy is moving both at once.

Revenue side
↑ Higher redemption rate
Pre-approved offers that reach consumers at the right moment convert at dramatically higher rates than cold direct mail — more funded loans from the same campaign spend. And when those loans close alongside non-interest income products like GAP, MRC, debt protection, and credit insurance, the revenue per funded loan multiplies significantly.
Cost side
↓ Lower campaign spend
Bureau prescreen pulls cost money per record — but so does everything downstream: mail file size, postage, printing, and list management. When we pre-filter who gets reached, every one of those line items shrinks. Smarter targeting reduces the entire cost of running a campaign, not just the bureau fee.
Cost Reduction

Cutting the cost of
the entire campaign.

Financial institutions pay bureau fees every time they pull a prescreen file — but that's just the start. Mail file size drives postage, printing, and list management costs too. Every record that doesn't convert is money spent on a dead end. Movemint reduces that waste by pre-filtering who gets reached, and by enabling consumers to initiate prequal themselves — shrinking the file, the mail, and the total spend all at once.

↓
↓ Cut prescreen cost
Smarter Prescreen

When a CU runs a direct mail campaign, they typically pull a broad prescreen file from the bureaus — everyone in their market above a credit score threshold. They pay per record pulled. Then they pay postage, printing, and list management fees on every piece that goes out. Most of those people never respond.

Movemint layers behavioral signals, intent data, and propensity scoring over that potential pool before the bureau pull happens. The CU submits a smaller, smarter file — and the savings cascade: fewer bureau fees, a smaller mail file, lower postage, lower print volume. Same campaign. Lower cost across every line item. Better hit rate.

Consumer-Initiated Prequal

Traditional prescreen is institution-initiated: the CU decides who to reach, pulls a file, and mails an offer. Consumer-initiated prequal flips the model — a consumer visits a landing page, a chatbot, or an in-app flow and asks what they're eligible for.

Because they opted in, the CU has a permissioned, intent-verified lead. In many cases, that's enough to bypass the prescreen pull entirely — no bureau fee for that consumer. And because the consumer already raised their hand, conversion rates are fundamentally higher than cold mail.
